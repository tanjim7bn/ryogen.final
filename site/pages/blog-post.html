How to choose between Japan and Malaysia for your degree

Two of the most popular destinations for students studying abroad are Japan and Malaysia. They suit different students for different reasons, and the right choice depends on your budget, your field, and the life you want while you study.

Cost of study

Tuition and cost of living vary widely between the two, and between universities within each. National universities often cost less than private ones, and scholarships can change the picture entirely.

A good shortlist starts with what you can realistically afford, then works up from there.

Language of instruction

Both destinations offer English taught programs, but the number and range differ by field. If you would rather not study in a new language, filter for English medium instruction early.

Scholarships

  • Government scholarships often cover full tuition and a monthly stipend
  • University awards range from partial waivers to merit grants
  • Deadlines are early, so plan backwards from them
